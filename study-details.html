BioWave™ Study — Detailed Evaluation

This page provides a detailed breakdown of how the BioWave™ stress regulation study was conducted, the student population involved, and qualitative feedback collected from participants during the evaluation phase.

Total Participants45 Students
Age Group18–24 years
Study DurationSingle Session
Primary MetricHRV

1. Student Population

The study involved 45 undergraduate students from diverse academic backgrounds. Participants were actively enrolled in college and reported frequent academic stress related to examinations, deadlines, and performance pressure.

Students with known cardiovascular, neurological, or autonomic disorders were excluded to avoid confounding physiological effects.

2. How the Study Was Conducted

The evaluation followed a within-subject design, meaning every student experienced both conditions:

  • Baseline condition (no wearable intervention)
  • BioWave™ active condition (NeuroHaptic™ stimulation enabled)

Each participant underwent a controlled cognitive stress task designed to simulate real academic pressure. Tasks included timed mental arithmetic, memory challenges, and countdown-based performance prompts.

3. Measurement & Observation

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) was continuously monitored using a wrist-based sensor integrated into the BioWave™ prototype. HRV was chosen because it directly reflects autonomic nervous system balance and stress recovery.

  • Lower HRV indicated higher stress
  • Rising HRV indicated recovery and calming

4. Key Observations

  • Average HRV increased by 22% within three minutes of activation
  • Autonomic recovery occurred 38% faster compared to baseline
  • Most participants showed improvement without conscious effort
